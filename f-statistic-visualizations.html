03 F-Distribution Curve

Where does your F fall under the null hypothesis?

The F-distribution with your observed F marked

The curve shows what F values look like when groups are actually identical. The red shaded region is the rejection zone at α = 0.05. Your F (green line) falling inside that region means the result is statistically significant.

05 Random Sampling Under H₀

If groups are truly equal, what F values do we see by pure chance?

1,000 random experiments where all groups have the same mean

Even when there's no real difference, F varies by chance. Most values cluster near 1; only a small tail goes above the critical value. That tail is what makes α = 0.05 meaningful.
